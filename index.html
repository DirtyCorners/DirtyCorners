Frequently Asked Questions

What is the idea behind “Dirty Corners”?

We need a community of good men and women to fight crime and misdeeds in out society - starting from where we live, work and commune. We need a place where we can effectively communicate such instances to our friends and family, colleagues and travelers. The idea is to raise awareness in our community about what happens, where and when. We care for others by keeping an eye on our community.

What can I find at “Dirty Corners”?

  • Crime reports such as robbery, burglary, thievery...
  • Traffic problems reports such as traffic jam, accident...
  • Reports for kidnap sightings, missing children, missing adults...

How does it work?

Why an app?

We want everyone to active in the cause. We facilitate all the possible means to enable everyone to report incidents wherever they may be, whichever device they use or whatever they may be doing at the moment. We provide apps for the following platforms:

  • iOS app
  • Android 4.4+ app
  • in-Facebook Canvas app
  • On-site app (visit http://app.dirtycorners.info/)

Why a fan page?

Our fan page servers dual purposes:

  • You can tag Dirty Corners in your Facebook shares to let us know
  • You can come to Dirty Corner Fan Page and find recent reports, warnings, precautions...

Why is it named “Dirty Corners”?

The name should give you the impression of dirty things (crime, traffic issues, inhumane acts...) you name it. It is the locations we should first pay attention to. Therefore “Corners”. How can “Dirty Corners” be useful to me?

Where do you gather information from?

The community.

How can I trust your reports?

We have special algorithms to validate your incident reports but you have to help us in the process. Depending on the kind of incident, we may need one or more to establish a community report. For a community report, individuals living in that area are (that could be you) asked to verify whether or not such an incident did happen. Depending on the feedbacks, reports should be regarded as trust worthy or not. Again, your valuable contribution to the cause is the key to the algorithm.

What kind of incidents can I report?

  • Crime: robbery, thievery, burglary...
  • Traffic problems reports such as traffic jam, accident...
  • Reports for kidnap sightings, missing children, missing adults...

What kind of incidents should I not report?

  • This is not a “Lost and Found” center. :D
  • Personal matters: This is not a site for personal matters or problems.
